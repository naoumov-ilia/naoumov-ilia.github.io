Two consecutive wave movements K1 and K2 may form "channel K1", provided that the necessary conditions for its construction are not violated. K1 is the primary movement, K2 is the secondary movement, with the duration of the secondary movement limited by the duration of the primary one: T2 ≤ T1. In turn, the secondary wave movement itself consists of a primary component K21 and a secondary component K22, which must satisfy the necessary conditions for channel construction, including the duration constraint T21 ≤ T1/2. We denote this channel as "channel K21".

In turn, the secondary wave movement of "channel K21" consists of a primary component K31 and a secondary component K32, which must satisfy the necessary conditions for channel construction, including the duration constraint T31 ≤ T21/2. We denote this channel as "channel K31". T32 ≤ T31. "Channel K41", ... and so on analogously as needed.

If the sufficient conditions for the impossibility of constructing "channel K31" are met for all T31 ≤ T21/2, then "channel K21" cannot be constructed; analogously, if the sufficient conditions for the impossibility of constructing "channel K21" are met for all T21 ≤ T1/2, then "channel K1" with the given f1, v1 cannot be constructed.

If at time t it turns out that "channel K31" cannot be constructed, then f31 = f31 + 1; if it can, then v31 = v31 + 1. If "channel K31" cannot be constructed for all T31 ≤ T21/2, then f21 = f21 + 1; otherwise v21 = v21 + 1, and we construct "channel K31". If channel K31 cannot be constructed with T31 ≤ T21/2 for any v21 such that T21 ≤ T1/2, then f1 = f1 + 1 and we continue constructing channel K21, K31, provided the necessary conditions for constructing the corresponding channels are not violated. If at the maximum value of {f1} the construction of "channel K1" is impossible, then v1 = v1 + 1, and we construct K21, K31, and so on. The increment fi = fi + 1 (or otherwise vi = vi + 1) is performed on the condition that it leads to a new fi greater than the previous maximum {fi}, for i = 1, 21, 31, ...
